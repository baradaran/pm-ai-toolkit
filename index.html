What PMs should actually use AI for

There's a lot of noise about "AI for PMs." Here's the honest take: use AI to compress the time between information and insight, and between insight and first draft. Keep the decisions.

📌 Snapshot — as of June 2026, based on current models and the latest reviews. The "delegate" / "keep human" split below reflects today's capabilities; models improve fast, so re-check before treating any limit as permanent. The accountability items ("you decide / you sign off") hold regardless.

✅ Delegate this (today)

  • Summarizing & clustering (tickets, interviews, threads)
  • First drafts — PRDs, updates, release notes
  • Triage, dedupe, severity-scoring at scale
  • Reframing & pressure-testing your thinking

⛔ Keep this human (judgment, not capability)

  • Deciding what to build and why
  • Real customer empathy & context only you have
  • Final numbers, commitments, anything shipped
  • Prioritization — it's judgment + strategy + politics
Rule of thumb: AI drafts; you decide. If the output leaves your hands, you reviewed it first.
